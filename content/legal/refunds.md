# CompeteIQ — Refund & Cancellation Policy

**Last updated:** 15 June 2026
**Effective date:** 15 June 2026

This Refund & Cancellation Policy explains how cancellations and refunds work for CompeteIQ subscriptions (the "Service"). It forms part of our [Terms of Service](../Terms/Terms-of-Service.md).

> **Note:** Template prepared for CompeteIQ's records and deployment; not legal advice.

---

## 1. Free report first

Every new account can generate a free report before paying, so you can evaluate CompeteIQ before subscribing. We encourage you to use it to decide whether the Service meets your needs.

## 2. Subscriptions and billing

Paid plans (Starter and Pro) are billed in advance on a recurring 30-day cycle in Australian dollars via Stripe. Each payment covers the upcoming period.

## 3. Cancelling

- You can cancel at any time from your billing settings or the Stripe customer portal.
- Cancellation stops future renewals. It takes effect at the **end of your current paid period** — you keep access until then.
- We do not provide pro-rata refunds for the unused part of a paid period that you simply choose to stop using, except where required by law (see section 5).

## 4. Refunds we offer

Because the free report lets you evaluate the Service first, paid periods are generally **non-refundable**. However, we will provide a refund in these cases:

- **Accidental or duplicate charge** — if you were charged in error or twice, we will refund the incorrect amount.
- **Billing after cancellation** — if you were charged for a renewal after validly cancelling, we will refund that charge.
- **Service failure** — if a sustained, significant fault on our side prevented you from using the Service for a paid period and we could not resolve it within a reasonable time, contact us and we will work out a fair refund or credit.

To request a refund, email **support.agent@agentmail.to** with your account email and the charge details. We aim to respond within 5 business days. Approved refunds are returned to your original payment method via Stripe (timing depends on your bank/card provider).

## 5. Your rights under the Australian Consumer Law

Our services come with guarantees that **cannot be excluded** under the Australian Consumer Law ("ACL"). Among other things, services must be provided with due care and skill and be fit for any purpose made known to us.

If we fail to meet a consumer guarantee, you may be entitled to a remedy — for a **major failure**, you may cancel and seek a refund or compensation; for a **minor failure**, we may choose to fix the problem (e.g. re-supply the service) within a reasonable time. Nothing in this Policy limits these rights. The remedies in section 4 are in addition to (not instead of) your ACL rights.

## 6. Price changes

If we change subscription prices, the change applies from your next renewal and will not affect a period you have already paid for. We will give reasonable notice of price increases.

## 7. Chargebacks

If you have a billing concern, please contact us first — we can usually resolve it faster than a chargeback. Initiating a chargeback for a legitimately incurred charge may result in suspension of your account pending resolution.
