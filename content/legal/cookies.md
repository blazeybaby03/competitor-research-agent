## 1. What cookies are

Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your browser. Similar technologies (such as local storage) work in comparable ways. They let a website remember your actions and preferences over time. We use only what is needed to run the Service securely.

## 2. Cookies we use

CompeteIQ is a lightweight tool and we keep cookie use to a minimum. We currently use:

**Strictly necessary cookies** — required for the Service to function and cannot be switched off in our systems:

- **Authentication & session** (set by Supabase) — keep you securely logged in and maintain your session.
- **Security** — help protect against unauthorised access and abuse.

**Payment cookies** — set by **Stripe** during checkout and billing to process payments securely and prevent fraud. These are set on Stripe's checkout/portal pages. See Stripe's privacy and cookie notices for details.

We do **not** currently use advertising cookies or sell data to advertisers. If we introduce analytics or other non-essential cookies in future, we will update this Policy and, where required, ask for your consent first.

## 3. First-party vs third-party cookies

- **First-party** cookies are set by CompeteIQ (e.g. our authentication session).
- **Third-party** cookies are set by our providers (e.g. Stripe for payments) when you interact with their components.

## 4. Managing cookies

You can control or delete cookies through your browser settings. Because our core cookies are strictly necessary, blocking them may stop you from logging in or using the Service. Browser help pages explain how to manage cookies for Chrome, Safari, Edge, and Firefox.

## 5. Do Not Track

Some browsers send "Do Not Track" signals. There is no agreed industry standard for responding to them, so we do not currently respond to DNT signals. As we use only essential cookies, this has limited practical effect.

## 6. Changes

We may update this Cookie Policy as the Service evolves. The "Last updated" date shows the latest version.
